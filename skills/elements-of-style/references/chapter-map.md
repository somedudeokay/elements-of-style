## Complete Rule List

| Rule | Optimized trigger | Original rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | possessive singular nouns | Form the possessive singular of nouns by adding 's |
| 2 | serial comma / Oxford comma | In a series of three or more terms with a single conjunction, use a comma after each term except the last |
| 3 | parenthetic commas and nonrestrictive clauses | Enclose parenthetic expressions between commas |
| 4 | comma before co-ordinate conjunction | Place a comma before a conjunction introducing a co-ordinate clause |
| 5 | comma splice / independent clauses | Do not join independent clauses by a comma |
| 6 | sentence fragments | Do not break sentences in two |
| 7 | dangling participles / opening modifiers | A participial phrase at the beginning of a sentence must refer to the grammatical subject |
| 8 | paragraph unity | Make the paragraph the unit of composition: one paragraph to each topic |
| 9 | topic sentence and paragraph coherence | As a rule, begin each paragraph with a topic sentence; end it in conformity with the beginning |
| 10 | active voice | Use the active voice |
| 11 | positive form | Put statements in positive form |
| 12 | concrete language | Use definite, specific, concrete language |
| 13 | concision | Omit needless words |
| 14 | loose sentence chains | Avoid a succession of loose sentences |
| 15 | parallel construction | Express co-ordinate ideas in similar form |
| 16 | word order and modifier placement | Keep related words together |
| 17 | tense consistency in summaries | In summaries, keep to one tense |
| 18 | emphasis by sentence position | Place the emphatic words of a sentence at the end |
